🔎 What to Listen For

As you listen, pay attention to: Energy and enthusiasm — does she sound excited? Specificity — does she give detailed answers or stay vague? Cultural fit — does she mention values that align with ours? Red flags — negativity about past employers, lack of interest in the role, short answers. Logistics — availability, pay expectations, start date.

💡 What to Listen For — Quick Reference

Green lights: Specific examples, questions about the team/culture, energy in their voice, clear motivation for applying, honest answers about weaknesses.

Yellow flags: Vague answers, talking only about pay/schedule, no questions asked, sounds distracted or multitasking.

Red flags: Badmouthing previous employers, can't explain job gaps, doesn't know what the role is, rude or dismissive tone, immediate salary demands with no interest in the role itself.

Insight The 5-Minute Truth
⏱️

You Don't Need 30 Minutes

Look at everything you just evaluated in a single short phone screen. You assessed energy, fit, availability, budget, brand excitement, red flags, and made a recommendation — all in under 10 minutes.

The truth is: sometimes we know within the first 5 minutes whether someone is going to be a good fit. The phone screen isn't about asking every question on the guide — it's about listening for the signals that tell you whether this person belongs on your team.

Trust your instincts, use the guide as a compass (not a script), and remember: a great 10-minute call beats a mediocre 30-minute one every time.

🏋️ Key Takeaways

> Phone screens are about listening, not interrogating.
> Resumes show qualifications — phone screens reveal character.
> Short, focused calls save time for you and the candidate.
> If the basics aren't met in 5 minutes, it's okay to wrap up respectfully.
> Always document your notes immediately after the call.
