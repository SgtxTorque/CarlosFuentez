The Discovery Conversation

The #1 Revenue Leak in Wireless Retail

Most reps jump straight to "What phone do you want?" instead of "What do you use your phone for?" Discovery drives: higher plans  |  more accessories  |  better retention

1
Connect First 30 sec
Build rapport before business. Acknowledge the person, not the transaction.
Say This:
"Welcome in! What brings you to T-Mobile today?" / "Hey! Shopping for yourself or someone else?"
Not This:
"Hi, are you looking for a new phone?"
Pro Tip:
Match their energy. Casual browser? Stay casual. Urgent need? Get focused fast.
2
Discover 2 – 4 min
Understand their life BEFORE you touch a device. Ask open-ended questions about how they actually use their phone.
Say This:
"Walk me through a typical day with your phone." / "What frustrates you most about your current setup?" / "Who else is on your plan? How do they use theirs?"
Not This:
"What phone are you interested in?"
Pro Tip:
Listen for: streaming, gaming, photos, travel, family, work. These are your selling cues.
3
Recommend 2 – 3 min
Connect THEIR needs to YOUR solution. Every recommendation ties back to something they said.
Say This:
"Your kids stream a lot — Go5G Plus gives unlimited premium data so they never buffer." / "Since you travel for work, this camera and battery life will make a real difference."
Not This:
"This is our best-selling phone."
Pro Tip:
Use "You said..." before every recommendation. It proves you listened.
4
Add Value 1 – 2 min
Accessories and protection aren't upsells — they solve problems the customer already told you about.
Say This:
"You drop your phone a lot — this case + protector saves you from a $250 repair." / "Always in the car? This MagSafe charger keeps you powered, no cord tangle."
Not This:
"Want a case with that?"
Pro Tip:
If you did discovery right, accessories sell themselves. No discovery = awkward upsell.
5
Close & Set Up Wrap-up
Confirm they feel good. Recap what they're getting and why. Transfer data. Leave them confident.
Say This:
"Let me recap — unlimited plan, new Galaxy with protection, and the car charger. Sound right?"
Not This:
"Anything else?"
Pro Tip:
Walk them to the door. Say their name. This earns the referral and the survey score.

Discovery Question Bank

Memorize 2–3 from each category. Rotate them so your conversations stay natural.

Lifestyle
  • Walk me through how you use your phone on a typical day.
  • What apps do you spend the most time on?
  • Do you stream music, shows, or games on your phone?
  • How important is your phone's camera to you?
Pain Points
  • What frustrates you most about your current phone?
  • Does your battery last through the day?
  • Are you happy with your current plan or feel like you're overpaying?
  • Any dead zones or coverage issues where you live or work?
Family & Plan
  • Who else is on your account? How do they use their devices?
  • Any kids who need their first phone or an upgrade?
  • Are you taking advantage of any family plan discounts?
  • Is anyone on your plan traveling internationally soon?
Accessories
  • How do you charge your phone — nightstand, car, office?
  • Have you ever cracked a screen or dropped your phone in water?
  • Do you use earbuds or a smartwatch with your phone?
  • What does your work-from-home or car setup look like?
Why Discovery Pays — The Math
Without Discovery
  • Avg Plan: Essentials — $60/mo
  • Device: Whatever they asked for
  • Accessories: $0 — didn't ask
  • Protection: Declined — wasn't framed
Avg Ticket: ~$60
With Discovery
  • Avg Plan: Go5G Plus — $90/mo
  • Device: Matched to their lifestyle
  • Accessories: Case + charger — $75
  • Protection: P360 — $18/mo (framed as savings)
Avg Ticket: ~$183+
3x Higher Ticket  •  Better Retention  •  Stronger Survey Scores

Same customer. Same store. The only difference is whether you asked the right questions first.

Remember
"What do you use your phone for?" > "What phone do you want?"
Ask first. Recommend second. The sale takes care of itself.
