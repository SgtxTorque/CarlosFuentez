### "The Operational Redesign" — Fitness Connection Transformation Project

This is one of the most impressive stories on the entire portfolio. It needs its own dedicated section on learning.html, positioned prominently (after the programs overview, before or after the H.Y.P.E. module showcase).

**THE STORY:**

Fitness Connection went through three different CEOs. One of those CEOs fundamentally changed how the company operated. The old model was a "jack and jill of all trades" approach where everyone could do everything, but nobody was truly responsible for anything. Club Managers couldn't answer a basic question: "What does a good shift look and feel like?" Everyone had a different answer.

The new model was a modified silo structure where every employee specialized in a role:
- **Fitness Professionals** were rebranded to two new specialized positions: **Connection Specialists** (Front Desk / Guest Experience) and **Fitness Consultants** (Membership Sales)
- **Assistant Managers** were split into **Operations Managers** and **Sales Managers**

Carlos was heavily involved in designing and executing this transformation. The rollout strategy was:
1. Select 5 pilot stores to facilitate the new program
2. Monitor their development closely
3. Train those 5 stores to become trainers themselves ("train the trainer")
4. Those trainers then trained 30+ additional locations
5. Creating trainers among trainers until every location could execute within a 60-day period

Carlos personally led several programs within this transformation, especially new TA practices, guest experience operations, and front desk operations. He created the deployment charts, station execution guides, shift cards, role-specific job aids, and training booklets that powered the rollout.

**The results:** Revenue started growing. Hiring practices improved. Shift alignment got better. Club Managers could finally answer the question "What does a good shift look and feel like?" with the same answer, because the system was designed to make good shifts the default.
