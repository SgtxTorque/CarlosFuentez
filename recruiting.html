Chapter 1

Amtel / T-Mobile Authorized Partner (2016-2022)

From 42 locations to 325+.

When I joined Amtel in 2016, there was no recruiting function. I was it. One person responsible for staffing an organization that was about to scale from 42 locations to 325+. I built everything from scratch: the process, the team, the tools, the employer brand.

I didn't just fill roles. I built the infrastructure. The ATS workflows. The interview frameworks. The employer brand. The careers site. The sourcing strategy. The onboarding integration. All of it.

Employer Brand

People wanted to work for us. I gave them a reason.

Chapter 2

Fitness Connection / Roark Capital (2022-2025)

Different company. Different challenge. Same results.

Fitness Connection had 40+ gym locations and an existing recruiting function, but it was stuck. The fitness industry only valued candidates from within, and they didn't understand that there was an entire market of transferable-skill candidates they were missing. I came in to transform how they identified and developed talent.

I led a team of 3, personally owned corporate and hard-to-fill roles, and built H.Y.P.E. (Hire Your Perfect Employee), a framework that reshaped how hiring managers ran structured interviews. The mindset shift was simple: stop looking for "gym industry people" and start building great employees from great people.

Revenue increased 5-15% quarter-over-quarter after operational changes took hold. The company hit its yearly revenue target for the first time in 5 years, resulting in a 125% payout.

But recruiting was never just recruiting for me. Better hiring fed directly into the operational redesign. When you hire the right people into the right roles, train them properly, and give them clear expectations, the business performs. That's why I was always involved in more than just filling seats.

Chapter 0

Fortune 500. High-growth. Full-cycle.

Before building in-house functions, I cut my teeth in contract and agency recruiting for Fortune 500 and high-growth organizations. Full-cycle recruiting across technology, pharma, healthcare, and corporate functions. Managed up to 180 requisitions simultaneously. Adapted training toolkits and process documentation to each client's structure.

The Approach

How I build recruiting functions.

I don't just post jobs. I build systems. Every recruiting function I've created follows the same core philosophy: make the process repeatable, measurable, and human.

Process Redesign

Map every step from requisition to first-day experience. Eliminate friction. Create accountability. Build for scale.

Structured Interviews

Replace gut-feel hiring with competency-based interview frameworks. Consistent, fair, and defensible.

Employer Brand Strategy

Build a brand that attracts the right people. Branded video, social presence, careers site, EVP messaging.

Direct Sourcing

Reduce agency dependency through proactive sourcing channels, referral programs, and community partnerships.

Onboarding Integration

Recruiting doesn't end at the offer letter. I design the handoff into onboarding to protect retention.

Data & Reporting

Build dashboards that track time-to-fill, source effectiveness, quality of hire, and retention by cohort.

The Takeaway

Recruiting isn't about filling seats. It's about building the foundation.

Every hire is a bet on the future of the organization. I build recruiting functions that don't just keep up with growth. They drive it. From 42 locations to 325+, from hourly turnover over 100% to under 70%, the numbers tell the story. But the real win is building something that works without you. A system that scales because it was built right from the start. I leave things better than I found them.
